# AI Safety Foundations

## What is AI Safety?

Artificial Intelligence (AI) Safety is the field of research focused on ensuring that AI systems behave in ways that are reliable, beneficial, and aligned with human values. As AI becomes more capable and autonomous, safety research aims to reduce harmful behavior, improve trustworthiness, and support responsible deployment.

---

## Why AI Safety Matters

AI systems are increasingly used in important domains such as healthcare, finance, education, cybersecurity, transportation, and scientific research.

Errors made by AI in these domains can have serious consequences. AI safety helps ensure that AI systems remain reliable, transparent, and accountable, especially when supporting high-impact decisions.

---

## Major AI Safety Challenges

### Hallucinations

AI models may generate information that appears convincing but is factually incorrect or unsupported.

### Goal Misalignment

An AI system may optimize for an objective that does not fully match human intentions.

### Reward Hacking

AI may exploit weaknesses in its reward function to achieve high scores without accomplishing the intended goal.

### Prompt Injection

Malicious instructions can manipulate AI systems into ignoring their intended behavior or revealing sensitive information.

### Data Poisoning

Training data may be intentionally modified to influence model behavior in unsafe ways.

### Model Drift

Over time, AI systems may behave differently because of changing environments, data distributions, or system updates.

---

## Current AI Safety Techniques

Researchers and organizations currently use several approaches to improve AI safety:

- Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (RLHF)
- Constitutional AI
- AI Red Teaming
- Safety Fine-Tuning
- Interpretability Research
- Responsible AI Frameworks
- Human-in-the-Loop Review

---

## Future Challenges

As AI systems become autonomous agents capable of making long-term decisions, new challenges emerge:

- Continuous monitoring after deployment
- AI agent supervision
- Trust evaluation over time
- Autonomous decision auditing
- Safe collaboration between multiple AI agents

---

## Conclusion

AI safety is no longer only about building better AI models. It also requires methods for continuously evaluating AI behavior after deployment.

This motivates the development of SentinelAI, which explores continuous AI safety monitoring as a research prototype.
